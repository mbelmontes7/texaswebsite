City Overview

One of the growing cities, Houston, is the fourth-largest in the U.S. Over 140 languages are spoken in Houston. It is also located in Southeast Texas near the Gulf of Mexico. Founded in 1836, Houston was named after General Sam Houston, who guaranteed Texas independence by winning the Battle of San Jacinto against Mexico. While Houston is known for its energy industry, especially oil and natural gas.

Region and Classification

Houston is an entry point to the Gulf Coast and the larger Southwest. Houston is classified as an urban metropolis with a wide range of houses, shops, and industrial areas. Houston's comprehensive layout is set apart by various communities and has a very complex highway structure. The city could be more friendly and walkable in some parts, but it needs some improvement in others. Average Income and Economy

Houston's average income shows the city's status as an enormous growing center for its people. As of 2022, the city's median household income was almos $70K. This income shows the city's wide economic landscape and it also ncludes strong industries including energy, medical services, manufacturing, and aerospace. Regardless of income level, Houston's relatively affordable rate of living makes it an appealing place for both businesses and people. The economy of the region is growing, fueled by a mix of local businesses, and international companies, and a growing entrepreneurial industry.
